# Publishing Sites

Share your Site with the Purroxy community and get a free account upgrade in return.

## What happens when you publish

- Your Site appears in the public library for anyone to install.
- Your account is upgraded to **Contributor** status (free forever). See [Pricing]({% link pricing.md %}).

## Step 1: Build your Site

Create a Site with at least one capability. Follow the [Getting Started]({% link getting-started.md %}) guide if you haven't built one yet. More capabilities means more value for other users.

## Step 2: Prepare for submission

Open your Site in the desktop app. Make sure it has a clear name and description. These are visible in the public library. Test each capability to confirm it works reliably.

## Step 3: Submit from the desktop app

Click **Submit to Library**. Purroxy packages your Site and sends it to the review queue. A public record is created on [GitHub](https://github.com/mreider/purroxy-sites) for transparency, but you do not need to use GitHub yourself.

{: .note }
> **What gets submitted?** Only metadata: name, description, capabilities, and automation definitions. **Credentials and session cookies are never included.** Your login stays on your machine.

## Step 4: Review

A maintainer reviews your Site and checks that:

- It works as described
- It targets a real, publicly accessible website
- Capabilities are clearly named and useful
- It does not violate the target site's Terms of Service

Most reviews finish within a few days. You will receive an email when your Site is approved or if changes are needed.

## Step 5: Contributor upgrade

Once approved, your Site goes live in the library and your account is upgraded to Contributor. If you had an active subscription, billing stops immediately with no partial charges.

## Tips

- **Pick a popular site.** Well-known services (insurance portals, government sites, CRMs) help more people.
- **Add multiple capabilities.** A Site with 3-5 capabilities is more valuable than one with a single action.
- **Write clear descriptions.** "Check claim status by claim number" is better than "do claim stuff."

## Or submit via GitHub PR

If you prefer working with Git, open a pull request on the [mreider/purroxy-sites](https://github.com/mreider/purroxy-sites) repository. Link your GitHub username in your Purroxy account settings so we can match the PR to your account. When merged, the same contributor upgrade applies.
